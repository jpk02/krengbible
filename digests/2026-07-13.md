## The Macro Read

Capital is abundant and electrons are scarce, and for a lender the interesting spread sits between those two facts.  US data center load is on a 31 → 41 → 66 GW path (2025–27), power availability — not capital — is the binding constraint, and financing that build-out is the dominant credit theme (Morgan Stanley frames an ~$800B private-credit opportunity in the 2025–28 data-center capex cycle).  The backdrop is a lender-friendly reset: spreads wider by 50–100 bps since late 2025, tighter docs, less leverage, fewer PIK, even as base rates ease (Fed toward ~3%, 10Y ~3.75–4.25%).  Today's flow rewards contracted, power-linked cash flow — Chevron underwriting 2.67 GW of dedicated gas for Microsoft, GIP/EQT taking AES private for contracted clean power — while the edge is discriminating 20-year IG offtake from development and merchant risk wearing an "infrastructure" label.

## Data Centers & Compute Power

**State of play.**  The hottest vertical in infrastructure and the price-setter for everything adjacent.  Goldman sees US data center load at 41 GW in 2026 and 66 GW by 2027; Morgan Stanley models ~$3T of AI infrastructure spend by 2028 with ~80% still ahead, and hyperscaler commitments toward $5T by 2030.  Blackstone's $85B data center platform is the largest in the world.  The constraint is physical — power and interconnection — so the action has moved to securing dedicated generation over leasing grid capacity.

**Today's moves.**
- **Chevron × Microsoft — "Project Kilby"** `[Confirmed]`.  20-year PPA for 2.67 GW of dedicated gas-fired generation near Pecos, TX; ~$7B est. cost, first power late 2028, FID targeted end-2026.  *Credit:* Microsoft is an IG, 20-year offtaker — bankable contracted cash flow; risk is construction/FID, not offtake.  Off-grid by design, bypassing the interconnection queue.  [Data Centre Magazine](https://datacentremagazine.com/news/chevron-and-microsoft-sign-2-67gw-data-centre-power-deal)
- **Alphabet × Intersect Power — $4.75B + debt** `[Confirmed, closed Mar 10]`.  Alphabet takes Intersect's ~8 GW development pipeline (operating assets spun to other investors); explicitly routing around utility bottlenecks.  *Credit:* pipeline becomes captive to Google — strong ultimate credit, but development-stage risk on individual projects.  [Cleary Gottlieb](https://www.clearygottlieb.com/news-and-insights/news-listing/alphabet-completes-4-75-billion-acquisition-of-intersect-power)
- **Hyperscale Data — 20 MW MSA, ~$1.2B (→~$3B at 52 MW)** `[Confirmed]`.  Michigan campus → undisclosed California neocloud; $5M upfront + $5.6M deposit; $100–120M fit-out; phased 10 MW Sept, 10 MW year-end.  *Credit:* unrated neocloud offtake + construction + single-tenant concentration → wide-end pricing absent enhancement.  Notable for reallocating ~28 MW from bitcoin mining — crypto-to-AI conversion.  [Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/company-news/hyperscale-data-signs-12b-ai-data-center-services-agreement-93CH-4757639)

**What to watch.**  Project Kilby FID by year-end; whether more oil majors follow with behind-the-meter gas; Virginia's $0.011/kWh data-center power tax as an opex drag on project DSCRs and a template for other states.

## Oil, Gas & Midstream

**State of play.**  Gas is the swing enabler of the AI build-out, and the curve reflects it.  Henry Hub has held a tight $3.15–3.34/MMBtu on strong supply (EIA sees ~$3.70 for 2026), but demand is firm: power burn hit ~45.6 Bcf/d in the first July heatwave — near the all-time record — and EIA sees power-sector gas up 2% in 2026 and 4% in 2027.  Layer on LNG export capacity climbing ~17 → ~19 Bcf/d and data centers adding 3+ Bcf/d by 2030: soft spot price, structurally rising demand — a setup that rewards infrastructure connecting supply to LNG and power load.

**Today's moves.**
- **Williams × Momentum Midstream — ~$5.5B** `[Reported — not final]`.  Williams (IG) buying from PE owner EnCap Flatrock; ~4,000 miles of pipe serving 10 LNG facilities and 26 power plants across the Haynesville/Gulf Coast.  Valuation is the eye-opener: **22–25× EBITDA vs. 10–14× for listed peers** (Momentum 2025–26 EBITDA ~$215–250M).  *Credit:* contracted midstream cash flow; watch the acquisition financing and leverage impact on WMB.  [TradingView](https://www.tradingview.com/news/gurufocus:232d8487f094b:0-williams-nears-5-5-billion-momentum-midstream-acquisition/)

**What to watch.**  A Williams announcement could land within a week — watch the financing package and whether the premium multiple re-rates comparable gathering assets.  Golden Pass and Corpus Christi Stage 3 train start-ups are the near-term demand catalysts.

Interconnection queues and equipment shortages are the choke point throttling both renewables and gas.  Deal-wise, the theme is funds and hyperscalers absorbing contracted generation aimed at tech load.

Asset base ~11.8 GW of contracted clean energy for tech customers.  *Credit:* one of the largest infra take-privates on record; watch pro-forma leverage on a now-private AES. **What to watch.**  Interconnection reform; storage attach-rates on new solar; whether merchant renewables can secure the tenant quality lenders now demand.
